Economic Impact

Creating Lasting Value: Economic Opportunity for Bahamians

📅 April 2025
✎ Sampson Cay
⌚ 6 min read

For Yntegra, the measure of a successful development extends well beyond the quality of the physical product. Sampson Cay is designed to generate meaningful and lasting socio-economic value for The Bahamas — a commitment that is embedded in the project's planning, its procurement strategies, and its vision for long-term operations.

Sampson Cay occupies a unique position in the landscape of Caribbean luxury development. Its scale, its partnership with Rosewood Hotels & Resorts, and its location in the Exumas make it a project of national significance for The Bahamas — not only as a symbol of the country's appeal to international investors, but as a genuine economic engine for communities that stand to benefit from its construction and operations over decades to come.

Employment Throughout Construction and Operations

The most direct economic contribution of Sampson Cay to Bahamian communities is employment. The project creates jobs across two distinct phases — the multi-year construction period, during which skilled tradespeople, engineers, logistics specialists, and support workers will be needed in significant numbers, and the ongoing operations phase, during which the resort, residences, and amenities will require a permanent and professional workforce.

Yntegra's commitment is to maximize Bahamian participation at both phases — not as a quota to be satisfied, but as a genuine priority that reflects the project's understanding of its obligations to the communities it operates within. This means active investment in workforce development: identifying skills needs, working with local training institutions, and building career pathways that give Bahamian workers the opportunity to grow into senior positions within the project's operations. For a broader view of Sampson Cay's development philosophy and community approach, see the project overview and values.

"Sampson Cay's success is understood to be inseparable from the prosperity of the Bahamian communities it operates within."

Engaging Bahamian Suppliers and Local Businesses

Beyond employment, Sampson Cay's economic contribution extends through its supply chains. Yntegra is committed to engaging Bahamian suppliers and local businesses wherever possible — in construction materials, food and beverage sourcing, maintenance services, professional services, and the many categories of goods and services that a development of this scale requires on an ongoing basis.

This approach to local procurement serves multiple purposes. It keeps more of the project's economic activity within the Bahamian economy rather than directing it abroad. It builds the capacity of Bahamian businesses to service large-scale international hospitality operations — capability that extends beyond Sampson Cay and strengthens the broader national economy. And it creates the kind of genuine, sustained relationships between a development and its local community that are the foundation of long-term social license and goodwill. Yntegra's engagement with Bahamian stakeholders is ongoing — follow updates on Yntegra's Facebook page.

Long-Term Socio-Economic Value for Exuma Communities

The communities of the Exumas are the immediate neighbors of Sampson Cay, and their long-term prosperity is central to the project's definition of success. Yntegra is engaged with Exuma communities not simply as stakeholders to be consulted, but as genuine partners whose knowledge of the local environment, culture, and economy is essential to the project's integrity and longevity.

This means supporting community infrastructure, contributing to educational and cultural initiatives, and ensuring that the presence of a world-class resort destination enhances rather than disrupts the fabric of Exuma community life. The aspiration for Sampson Cay is not to exist as an island unto itself — in the literal or figurative sense — but to be integrated into the Exumas as a positive and generative presence that reflects well on The Bahamas as a whole. The project's sustainable approach to island development reinforces the same values of longevity and responsibility that guide its community engagement.

Conclusion

The case for responsible luxury development in The Bahamas ultimately rests on its ability to deliver genuine value to the country — not only in the form of international recognition and high-end tourism, but in the employment, business development, and community investment that translate a major project into a lasting national asset. Sampson Cay is designed with exactly this aspiration in mind. For Yntegra and Rosewood, a development that does not create real and lasting opportunity for Bahamians is simply not aligned with their shared vision of what this project should be.
